Enhanced Plant Disease Detection Using DL

The identification and restoration of plant diseases is a critical challenge in modern agriculture, directly impacting food security and crop sustainability. Our method presents a robust and efficient solution for automatically detecting plant diseases and recommending restoration measures, utilizing the Xception architecture a deep convolutional neural network (CNN) model known for its superior classification capabilities. We train our model on the PlantVillage dataset, encompassing 39 classes, including 38 plant disease categories and one background class. To extend the utility of disease detection, we integrate a real-time chatbot assistant powered by an extensive agricultural knowledge base, offering users instant guidance on crop management, disease prevention, and treatment strategies. Furthermore, we introduce an integrated store platform that directs users to recommended agricultural products such as pesticides and medications tailored to the diagnosed disease. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of our Xception-based system, achieving high accuracy in disease identification while offering a seamless user experience for practical restoration. By leveraging deep learning and intelligent interfaces, our approach empowers farmers and agriculturists to maintain healthier crops and enhance agricultural productivity.
